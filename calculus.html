Lesson 1- Factoring By GCF

Okay so the first thing I will be teaching is factoring the Greatest Common Factor which in short is called GCF

Factor: Two or more #'s or variables that are mutiplied to create a term.

Example:Factors of 14 would be 7,2,1 and 14. This is because 14 * 1 equals 14 and 7 * 2 equals 14. Also some of you might think your smart and use decimals but with factors it can only be a whole number.
